Retro Nokia with changeable covers and Snake

Back in the late 90’s, Nokia released the 5110 with X-Press On covers and the snake game. I desperately wanted it but when I was a teenager it was extremely rare for kids to have mobiles.

The TV advert used a famous piece of Spanish guitar music that included a couple of bars that became Nokia’s infamous start up sound. I’d just learnt how to play the piece on my guitar and thought this might convince my parents to buy me the phone.

It didn’t work. But twenty years later, I built the snake game in a Scrimba tutorial and thought I could put it inside the phone again. And have the music play in the background. Finally, some ‘closure’.

Project Requirements

MVP

  • Design five X-Press On covers
  • Build photo carousel for the covers
  • Place Snake game inside the phone
  • Create a scoreboard
  • Include an option to play music

Next Steps

  • Connect YouTube via its API, rather than an embed.

Process

Step 1

Like many others, to learn more about arrays I followed a tutorial to build 'Snake'.

The tutorial had no styling though, so I wanted to add to it and practice CSS and more JavaScript.

First, I had to draw an old Nokia 5110 and add a few different colours.

Step 2

Just before Snake I’d learnt how to build a photo carousel in Vanilla JS, so this seemed like a good opportunity to combine them.

Five phones would've taken too much space all in a line, so I turned to Google to find how to transform them. And add some animation.

Having learnt about classes and prototypes, trying to adapt, re-build and understand the demo that I found was good practice.

Step 3

Putting Snake inside the phone was simple enough, but making it responsive required a few media queries. I settled on using relative and absolute position, with a z-index.

Using the same techinque I wanted the start button to be on top of the phone's home key. Works in Chrome, but in Safari you can see it's slipped down.

Step 4

I initially placed a scoreboard to the left, to record current and high scores. On landscape orientation it sits to the side of the phones in a flexbox.

On smaller screens I switched the flexbox to column, but it was poor UX to scroll up and down. A future plan is to have the score inside the phone too, displaying high scores at Game Over.

Step 5

I really want the option of Tarrega’s Gran Vals to play during the game, with a mute button for those who don’t want to listen.

To avoid breaching copyright I’ve taken the music from a YouTube video.

For the MVP I’ve just embedded YouTube in an iFrame, and initially put it to the left of the phones.

But again, on small screens it disappears below. I really need to add the YouTube API and play/pause/stop/mute buttons

Bugs and Solutions

Quite a few CSS layout issues here:

  • Putting the Snake game inside was easy. But the blue start button won't stay still. Need to re-look at what's going on with the percentages; they should all scale...
  • I added a fade in/out transition to the snake game when the phone covers change. I’ve added the same to the play button, but that doesn’t work.
  • Complete overhaul of the design is needed. The 'About' section could be on another page, to save a scroll. The scoreboard needs to sit with snake in the phone.
  • On Game Over the snake grid should be replaced by a High Score list. Simple array; also show highest score of the day; keep it in local storage.
  • The YouTube API would give greater control over play/pause/stop buttons, and let the YouTube video be irrelevant.
  • Text-shadows aren't great and there's an opacity/alpha issue with the five phones.
