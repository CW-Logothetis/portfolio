Retro Nokia with changeable covers and Snake

Back in the late 90’s, Nokia released the 5110 with X-Press On covers and the snake game. I desperately wanted it but when I was a teenager it was extremely rare for kids to have mobiles.

The TV advert used a famous piece of Spanish guitar music that included a couple of bars that became Nokia’s infamous start up sound. I’d just learnt how to play the piece on my guitar and thought this might convince my parents to buy me the phone.

It didn’t work. But twenty years later, I built the snake game in a Scrimba tutorial and thought I could put it inside the phone again. And have the music play in the background. Finally, some ‘closure’.

Project Requirements

MVP

  • Snake game - Vanilla JS
  • Design five X-Press On covers
  • Build photo carousel for the covers
  • Place Snake game inside the phone
  • Create a scoreboard
  • Include an option to play music

Next Steps

  • Switch from Flexbox to Grid
  • Place scoreboard inside phone, to toggle on at Game Over
  • Connect YouTube via its API, rather than an embed.

Process

Step 1

Like many others, I followed a tutorial to learn more about arrays by building Snake.

I wanted to add more to it though, to practice CSS and more JavaScript.

Step 2

Just before Snake I’d learnt how to build a photo carousel in Vanilla JS, so this seemed like a good opportunity to combine them.

They’d have taken too much space all in a line, so I turned to Google to find how to transform them. And add some animation.

Step 3

Putting Snake inside the phone presented a bit of a challenge responsively. I settled on using position relative and absolute, with a z-index.

Step 4

The scoreboard was fairly straightforward. On landscape orientation it sits to the side of the phones in a flexbox.

On smaller screens the flexbox switches to column, but it’s not a good user experience to scroll. A future plan is to have the score inside the phone too, displaying at Game Over.

Step 5

I really want the option of Tarrega’s Gran Vals to play during the game, with a mute button for those who don’t want to listen.

To avoid breaching copyright I’ve taken the music from a YouTube video.

For the MVP I’ve just embedded youTube in an iFrame, in the flexbox to the left of the phones.

Again, on small screens this isn’t a great user experience.

Bugs and Solutions

Quite a few CSS layout issues here:

  • Putting the Snake game inside was easy. Until I resized the screen. I had hoped percentages would move everything responsively, but I’ve not made it work. So a few media queries are needed here.
  • I added a fade in/out transition to the snake game when the phone covers change. I’ve added the same to the play button, but that doesn’t work.
  • Complete overhaul of the design. A move to grid would help. And for smaller screens, removing the scoreboard and YouTube completely.
  • The YouTube API would give greater control over play/pause/stop buttons. Plus to play the song automatically when the game starts, or to mute it.
